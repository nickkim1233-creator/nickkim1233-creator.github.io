Where to buy & trade crypto

Three main places. Think of them like different types of financial institutions — each with real trade-offs.

🏦
Type 01
Centralized Exchange

A company holds your crypto, like a bank holds dollars. Sign up with email and password — they handle security for you.

CoinbaseKrakenBinanceGemini
Easiest start — sign up and buy
Account recovery if locked out
Bank transfers and card deposits
You don't truly own the coins
Can freeze accounts; regulated
⚙️
Type 02
Decentralized Exchange

Trade directly from your own wallet. No company, no signup. Smart contracts run everything automatically.

UniswapJupiterRaydiumCurve

Access to thousands of tokens

Self-Custody Wallet

An app giving total ownership. You get a "seed phrase" (12–24 words) — the master key. Lose it and your funds are gone forever.

PhantomMetaMaskLedgerTrust Wallet

No customer support

Part 02

Moving crypto without losing it

This is where most mistakes happen. Read the first section before anything else.

🧠
The one concept you must understand first
Read before any transfer
▾

Think of an address like a phone number, and the blockchain like a phone network. The same coin can travel on different networks — and each network requires a different address format.

⚠
Sending a coin on the wrong network is the #1 way people permanently lose funds. Your coin reaches a valid address — but on the wrong network, so it's unreachable forever.

Simple · Bitcoin network

🌀
Scenario 2 — BTC via Solana: Coinbase → Phantom
Tricky · Wrapped tokens
▾
⚠
Sending BTC "via Solana" means you're not sending real Bitcoin. You're sending cbBTC — a Solana token tracking Bitcoin's value. You need a Solana address, not a Bitcoin address.

Why? Bitcoin takes 10–60 min and costs $2–20 in fees. Solana: under 1 second, $0.001.

1
Open Phantom → switch to Solana
Your Solana address is 44 characters of mixed letters/numbers. No "bc1" prefix.
2
On Coinbase, select cbBTC (not regular BTC)
This is a different asset from standard Bitcoin.
3
Set network to Solana → paste Solana address → send
The address must be your 44-character Solana address — not the bc1q address.
Bitcoinbc1qar0srrr7xfkvy5l643lydnw9re59gtzzwf5mdq
Solana7XkrMYT5gvp1B9YLpFWpRxDtCvPqY3tPdZbKrjVfQP
!
Never paste a Bitcoin address when the network is Solana. Funds are unrecoverable.
💎
Scenario 3 — ETH: Coinbase → MetaMask
Ethereum and its side-networks
▾
→
MetaMask's 0x address is the same on all EVM chains. But sending via Polygon means it arrives on Polygon — not mainnet. They're separate balances.
1
Copy your MetaMask address (0x…)
Same address on Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, Polygon, and more.
2
Choose your network on Coinbase carefully
Ethereum mainnet = slower, pricier. Base/Arbitrum/Polygon = fast, cheap.
3
In MetaMask, switch to the same network
Your ETH will appear there once confirmed.
✓
Wrong EVM network? Recoverable — just add that network to MetaMask and switch to it.
🪙

Pre-send checklist
▾
01What coin am I sending? Name it — BTC, ETH, USDC, SOL.
02What network am I sending on? Not automatic — you choose.
03Does the receiving wallet support that network?
04Is the address format correct for that network?
05Did I send a small test amount first?
06Did I verify the first and last 4 characters of the address?

Part 01

Where to buy
& trade crypto

Three main places. Think of them like different types of financial institutions — each with real trade-offs.

🏦
Type 01
Centralized Exchange

A company holds your crypto, like a bank holds your dollars. Sign up with email and password — they handle security for you.

CoinbaseKrakenBinanceGemini
Easiest start — just sign up and buy
Account recovery if you get locked out
Bank transfers and card deposits
You don't truly own the coins (the company does)
Can freeze accounts; subject to regulation
⚙️
Type 02
Decentralized Exchange

Trade directly from your own wallet. No company, no signup. Smart contracts run the exchange automatically.

UniswapJupiterRaydiumCurve
You hold your coins at all times
Access to thousands of tokens
No customer support — mistakes are permanent
Requires a self-custody wallet first
👻
Type 03
Self-Custody Wallet

An app you install. You receive a "seed phrase" (12–24 words) — the master key. Lose it and your funds are gone. No exceptions.

PhantomMetaMaskLedgerTrust Wallet
Complete ownership — only you control it
Connects to any DEX
Lost seed phrase = permanent loss, no recovery
No customer support whatsoever
→
Where to start: Buy on a centralized exchange like Coinbase. Once comfortable, move some to Phantom to explore DEXs and full ownership.
Part 02

Moving crypto
without losing it

This is where most mistakes happen. Read the first section before anything else — it explains the concept behind every scenario.

🧠
The one concept you must understand first
Read before any transfer
▾

Think of an address like a phone number, and the blockchain like a phone network. The same coin can travel on different networks — and each network requires a different address format.

⚠
Sending a coin on the wrong network is the #1 way people permanently lose funds. Your coin reaches a valid address — but on the wrong network, so it's unreachable forever.

Scenario 2 — BTC via Solana: Coinbase → Phantom
Tricky · Wrapped tokens
▾
⚠
Sending BTC "via Solana" means you're not sending real Bitcoin. You're sending cbBTC — a Solana token tracking Bitcoin's value. You need a Solana address, not a Bitcoin address.

Why do this? Bitcoin takes 10–60 min and costs $2–20 in fees. Solana takes under 1 second and costs $0.001.

1
Open Phantom → switch to Solana
Your address is a 44-character string of mixed letters/numbers. No "bc1" prefix.
2
Copy your Solana address
Example: 7XkrMYT5gvp1B9YLpFWp…
3
On Coinbase, select cbBTC (not regular BTC)
This is a different asset from standard Bitcoin (BTC).
4
Select Solana as the network → paste → send
The address must be your 44-character Solana address — not the bc1q Bitcoin address.
Bitcoinbc1qar0srrr7xfkvy5l643lydnw9re59gtzzwf5mdq
Solana7XkrMYT5gvp1B9YLpFWpRxDtCvPqY3tPdZbKrjVfQP
⚠ WrongBitcoin address on Solana network = lost forever
!
Never paste a Bitcoin address when the network is Solana. Funds go somewhere unreachable and cannot be recovered.
💎
Scenario 3 — ETH: Coinbase → MetaMask
Ethereum and its side-networks
▾

MetaMask uses the same address across multiple networks. But which network you send on determines where your balance appears.

→
Your MetaMask 0x address is identical on Ethereum, Polygon, Arbitrum, and Base. Sending via Polygon means it arrives on Polygon — not mainnet.
1
Copy your MetaMask address (starts with 0x…)
Same address works on Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, Polygon, and more.
2
Choose your network on Coinbase
Ethereum mainnet = slower, more expensive. Base, Arbitrum, Polygon = faster, cheap.
3
In MetaMask, switch to the matching network
Your ETH will appear there once confirmed.
✓
Wrong EVM network? You can recover it — just add that network to MetaMask and switch to it.
🪙

Every Time

Pre-send
checklist

01What coin am I sending? Name it — BTC, ETH, USDC, SOL. Sounds obvious; do it anyway.
02What network am I sending on? Bitcoin? Ethereum? Solana? Selected at the sending platform — not automatic.
03Does the receiving wallet support that network? Phantom = Solana + Bitcoin. MetaMask = Ethereum + EVM chains. Never assume.
04Is the address format correct? Bitcoin = bc1q/1/3. Solana = long Base58. ETH/EVM = 0x…
05Did I test send first? Always. Send $1–5, confirm arrival, then send the rest.
06Did I verify the first and last 4 characters? Clipboard hijacking malware is real. Check manually every time.
